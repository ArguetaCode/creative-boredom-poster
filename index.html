THE TRUTH ABOUT
CREATIVE BOREDOM

We usually hate feeling bored, especially at work or during long days. However, researchers say that a little boredom can actually help our creativity. When our mind is not full of messages, videos, and notifications, it has space to play with new ideas. KEY IDEA: Simple, boring activities can give your brain time to explore new connections. When you are not focused on a screen, your imagination can start working in the background.
Try this: Sit quietly for five minutes and just look out of the window. Let your thoughts move slowly. Do not touch your phone. See what new ideas appear.

GOOD WAYS TO USE BOREDOM

Use short moments of boredom as a tool. Choose calm, low-energy activities that leave space for your mind to think:

  • Doing repetitive tasks like washing, cleaning, or organizing.
  • Sitting quietly or looking out of the window without your phone.
  • Writing down new ideas, dreams, or plans for the future.
BAD WAYS TO BE BORED

Some habits stop your creativity and only make you feel more tired:

  • Endlessly scrolling through social media without a real purpose.
  • Complaining about boredom but not trying anything different.
  • Doing nothing for hours and waiting for inspiration to magically appear.
